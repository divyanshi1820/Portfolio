Overview

This holistic approach ensures that each project is handled with a high level of detail and professionalism.

Concept & Process

The concept stems from the idea of a “second skin”—a protective exoskeleton that shelters the research modules from harsh wind, snow accumulation, and thermal loss. The form evolves through parametric design using Grasshopper, adapting to functional zoning, terrain contours, and solar orientation.

Interior modules are suspended inside this exoskeleton, allowing wind to pass beneath and around, while maintaining thermal isolation. Modular clusters follow a programmatic spine: residential, laboratory, operations, and logistics, with utility cores strategically placed for maintenance efficiency.
