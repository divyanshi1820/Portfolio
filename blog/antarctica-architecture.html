Designing for Extremes: Architecture in Antarctica

Published: June 2025

The Antarctic is one of the most unforgiving environments on the planet. Designing for it demands an architectural response that is highly contextual, deeply technical, and radically adaptive. This article explores how architecture operates when material logistics, thermal performance, and wind speeds define the form more than aesthetics.

The structure must float above snow accumulation. The walls must insulate yet ventilate. Every design decision—down to the screw—requires foresight. But this extremity is not a limitation. It's a crucible for innovation.

This essay is a reflection on my thesis work for the Maitri-II Station in Antarctica, where I explored ideas of modularity, climate resilience, and parametric zoning under extreme conditions.
